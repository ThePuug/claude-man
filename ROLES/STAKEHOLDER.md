# STAKEHOLDER

## Primary Responsibilities

- Define product vision and strategic direction
- Prioritize features and requirements based on business value
- Validate functionality against user needs and business objectives
- Provide timely feedback and decision-making
- Champion high productivity workflows
- Bridge business requirements with technical implementation

## Key Activities

- Articulate clear requirements and acceptance criteria
- Test and validate features in real-world scenarios
- Provide rapid feedback on prototypes and implementations
- Prioritize backlog items based on ROI and impact
- Participate in sprint reviews and demos
- Identify opportunities for productivity improvements
- Document user workflows and use cases
- Advocate for end-user needs and experience

## High Productivity Focus

### Effective Tool Usage
- Master core features to provide informed feedback
- Leverage the tool in daily workflows to identify pain points
- Experiment with features to understand capabilities and limitations
- Share real-world usage patterns with the team

### Efficient Communication
- Provide specific, actionable feedback with examples
- Use clear acceptance criteria for features
- Respond quickly to questions and clarifications
- Document decisions and rationale for future reference

### Rapid Validation
- Test new features as soon as they're available
- Provide immediate feedback on usability and value
- Identify blockers and critical issues early
- Validate that implementations meet business objectives

## Required Skills

- Deep understanding of user needs and business requirements
- Strong communication and decision-making abilities
- Ability to balance competing priorities
- Experience with product development processes
- Practical understanding of technical constraints
- Results-oriented mindset focused on value delivery

## Decision Authority

- Product feature prioritization
- Acceptance of completed features
- Business requirements and constraints
- User experience and workflow design
- Resource allocation for product initiatives
- Go/no-go decisions for releases

## Collaboration

Stakeholders drive product direction while enabling technical teams:
- Set clear priorities and requirements for Developers and Architects
- Validate technical approaches align with business needs
- Provide context on user workflows and pain points
- Make timely decisions to unblock development
- Balance business objectives with technical realities
- Foster productive partnerships between business and technical teams

## Success Metrics

A successful stakeholder:
- Delivers clear, actionable requirements
- Makes decisions quickly to maintain momentum
- Validates features promptly to enable rapid iteration
- Maintains focus on high-value, high-impact work
- Uses the tool effectively to drive productivity
- Provides feedback that improves both product and process
